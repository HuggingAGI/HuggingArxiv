# 适应网络威胁：一个用于网络钓鱼生成及利用大型语言模型分析其进化模式的网络钓鱼进化网络（PEN）框架

发布时间：2024年11月18日

`LLM应用` `网络安全` `网络钓鱼`

> Adapting to Cyber Threats: A Phishing Evolution Network (PEN) Framework for Phishing Generation and Analyzing Evolution Patterns using Large Language Models

# 摘要

> 网络钓鱼始终是一种广泛存在的网络威胁，攻击者精心炮制欺骗性邮件，诱使受害者泄露敏感信息。尽管人工智能（AI），尤其是深度学习，已成为抵御网络钓鱼攻击的关键要素，但这些方法存在关键局限。因隐私问题，公开可用、多样且更新的数据稀缺，极大地制约了其有效性。随着网络钓鱼策略迅速演变，基于有限、过时数据训练的模型难以侦测新的、复杂的欺骗策略，导致系统易受越来越多的攻击。填补这一差距对于在愈发恶劣的网络环境中强化防御至关重要。为解决此差距，我们提出了网络钓鱼进化网络（PEN），这是一个借助大型语言模型（LLM）和对抗训练机制持续生成高质量、逼真且多样化网络钓鱼样本的框架，并对LLM提供的网络钓鱼特征进行分析，以了解不断变化的网络钓鱼模式。我们评估了PEN生成的网络钓鱼样本的质量和多样性，发现其生成的逼真网络钓鱼样本超过80%，有效拓展了七种主要类型的网络钓鱼数据集。这些由PEN生成的样本提升了当前网络钓鱼探测器的性能，使检测准确率提高40%。此外，PEN的运用显著增强了模型的稳健性，将探测器对干扰的敏感度降低多达60%，从而降低了对抗条件下的攻击成功率。当我们分析LLM生成的网络钓鱼所采用的模式时，与现有网络钓鱼相比，认知复杂度和时间限制的语气在统计上存在显著差异。

> Phishing remains a pervasive cyber threat, as attackers craft deceptive emails to lure victims into revealing sensitive information. While Artificial Intelligence (AI), particularly deep learning, has become a key component in defending against phishing attacks, these approaches face critical limitations. The scarcity of publicly available, diverse, and updated data, largely due to privacy concerns, constrains their effectiveness. As phishing tactics evolve rapidly, models trained on limited, outdated data struggle to detect new, sophisticated deception strategies, leaving systems vulnerable to an ever-growing array of attacks. Addressing this gap is essential to strengthening defenses in an increasingly hostile cyber landscape. To address this gap, we propose the Phishing Evolution Network (PEN), a framework leveraging large language models (LLMs) and adversarial training mechanisms to continuously generate high quality and realistic diverse phishing samples, and analyze features of LLM-provided phishing to understand evolving phishing patterns. We evaluate the quality and diversity of phishing samples generated by PEN and find that it produces over 80% realistic phishing samples, effectively expanding phishing datasets across seven dominant types. These PEN-generated samples enhance the performance of current phishing detectors, leading to a 40% improvement in detection accuracy. Additionally, the use of PEN significantly boosts model robustness, reducing detectors' sensitivity to perturbations by up to 60%, thereby decreasing attack success rates under adversarial conditions. When we analyze the phishing patterns that are used in LLM-generated phishing, the cognitive complexity and the tone of time limitation are detected with statistically significant differences compared with existing phishing.
