# SciAgents：利用多智能体智能图推理，自动化科学发现过程

发布时间：2024年09月09日

`Agent` `材料科学` `人工智能`

> SciAgents: Automating scientific discovery through multi-agent intelligent graph reasoning

# 摘要

> 在人工智能领域，一个重大挑战是如何构建能够自主探索新领域、识别复杂模式并揭示科学数据中隐藏联系的系统。为此，我们推出了 SciAgents，这一方法融合了三大核心技术：（1）利用大规模本体知识图谱来整合和连接多样化的科学概念；（2）结合大型语言模型（LLMs）和数据检索工具；（3）采用具备现场学习能力的多代理系统。应用于生物启发材料研究时，SciAgents 揭示了以往被忽视的跨学科关联，其探索能力和精度远超传统研究方法。该框架不仅能自主生成和优化研究假设，还能阐明材料背后的机制、设计原则及意外特性。通过模块化整合这些功能，SciAgents 不仅推动了新材料发现，还对现有假设进行批判性分析和改进，同时提供最新研究数据的检索，并评估其优缺点。案例研究显示，SciAgents 成功融合了生成性 AI、本体表示和多代理建模，形成类似生物系统的“智能群”，为材料科学开辟了新路径，并加速了先进材料的研发进程。

> A key challenge in artificial intelligence is the creation of systems capable of autonomously advancing scientific understanding by exploring novel domains, identifying complex patterns, and uncovering previously unseen connections in vast scientific data. In this work, we present SciAgents, an approach that leverages three core concepts: (1) the use of large-scale ontological knowledge graphs to organize and interconnect diverse scientific concepts, (2) a suite of large language models (LLMs) and data retrieval tools, and (3) multi-agent systems with in-situ learning capabilities. Applied to biologically inspired materials, SciAgents reveals hidden interdisciplinary relationships that were previously considered unrelated, achieving a scale, precision, and exploratory power that surpasses traditional human-driven research methods. The framework autonomously generates and refines research hypotheses, elucidating underlying mechanisms, design principles, and unexpected material properties. By integrating these capabilities in a modular fashion, the intelligent system yields material discoveries, critique and improve existing hypotheses, retrieve up-to-date data about existing research, and highlights their strengths and limitations. Our case studies demonstrate scalable capabilities to combine generative AI, ontological representations, and multi-agent modeling, harnessing a `swarm of intelligence' similar to biological systems. This provides new avenues for materials discovery and accelerates the development of advanced materials by unlocking Nature's design principles.
